About Our Airdrie

Why this exists, and what's next.

What this is

Our Airdrie pulls together local business listings, a community events calendar, free programs and civic resources, and daily weather and road closure updates - all in one searchable site. Every business listing comes straight from the City of Airdrie's own licensing records, cross-checked against the Chamber of Commerce directory, so it's accurate and current — not scraped from social media.

Built for Airdrie first

Not a generic national directory. Every listing, every event is local — and built to grow with the city as it heads toward 100,000 residents.

What's next

More businesses claiming and updating their own listings, real-time event submissions from local organizations, and deals that residents can actually use. If you want to help shape it, reach out on the Contact tab.

Frequently asked questions

Is this an official City of Airdrie site?
No. Our Airdrie is an independent community project. The business directory pulls from the City of Airdrie's public licence records and the Airdrie Regional Chamber of Commerce directory, but this site isn't run or endorsed by either.
Is it free to use?
Yes, for residents it's free to browse, search, and see events — always. Businesses can list for free too.
How do I add or fix my business listing?
Use the "Add a business we're missing" form on the Contact tab to report a new listing, or use the same form to flag corrections to an existing one.
Where does the event information come from?
Events are pulled from public community calendars (like DiscoverAirdrie.com) and event listing sites. It's a periodic snapshot, not a live feed, so always confirm details with the event organizer before heading out.
Is the search box really AI?
Honestly, no — it's rule-based keyword matching against the directory data, not a hosted AI model. It's built to handle common phrasing like "I need a plumber" or "what's happening this weekend," but it won't understand everything a real AI assistant would.
What's actually on this site?
Four things: a searchable directory of ... local businesses, a calendar of community events, a list of free and low-cost programs (subsidies, free courses, newcomer support, parks), and daily weather with current road closures - all on one site.
How is my privacy protected?
You can browse the entire directory, calendar, and resources without creating an account or submitting any personal information. If you do use one of our forms, we only use what you submit to respond to your request - we never sell your information. Full details are in our Privacy Policy.

Parks & outdoor spaces

Chinook Winds Regional Park

55+ acres with 2km of paved pathways. Three playgrounds, a skate park, spray park, beach volleyball, ball diamonds, a winter toboggan hill, and seasonal concessions.

Nose Creek Regional Park

20 acres right in the downtown core. Amphitheatre, concession area, fire pit, picnic tables, a covered shelter, and nature trails.

Fletcher Regional Park

A playground, three shale ball diamonds, and a nine-hole disc golf course.

Airdrie maintains 90 playgrounds in total across parks and school sites - see the full parks map →
