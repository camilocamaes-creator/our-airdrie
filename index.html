About Our Airdrie

Why this exists, and what's next.

What this is

Our Airdrie is a single place for Airdrie residents to find what they need without hunting across five different websites. Type a question in plain English, like "when's the next bus to Calgary," "what day is recycling pickup in Windsong," "plumber near me," or "what's on this weekend," and get an answer, not a link to a PDF.

The site covers a searchable business directory, a community events calendar, transit schedules for all 6 Airdrie routes, garbage and recycling pickup by neighbourhood, free programs and civic resources, and daily weather. Everything is specific to Airdrie, with no generic national filler.

Transit: schedules on screen, no downloads

All 6 Airdrie Transit routes are built directly into the site: local routes 1 and 3, Route 900 to CrossIron Mills and the Rundle LRT, and the ICE express routes 901 and 902 to downtown Calgary. Tap any route from the Transit tab or search for it, and the full schedule opens right on the page. No PDF required.

Garbage and recycling by neighbourhood

Pick your neighbourhood from the list (all 44 are covered) and see your collection day, what's collected each week, and the schedule for large item pickup and organic waste carts. The Garbage and Recycling tab has it all in one place.

Built for Airdrie first

Not a generic national directory. Every listing, event, and schedule is local, built to grow with the city as it heads toward 100,000 residents.

What's next

More events as local organizations submit them, more free programs as we find them, and richer answers to the questions residents ask most. If something's missing or wrong, reach out on the Contact tab.

Frequently asked questions

Is this an official City of Airdrie site?
No. Our Airdrie is an independent community project. The business directory is built from public source data.
Is it free to use?
Yes. It's always free for residents to browse, search, and see events. Businesses can list for free too.
How do I add or fix my business listing?
Use the "Add a business we're missing" form on the Contact tab to report a new listing, or use the same form to flag a correction to an existing one.
Where does the event information come from?
Events are pulled from public community calendars and event listing sites. It's a periodic snapshot, not a live feed, so always confirm details with the organizer before heading out.
Is the search box really AI?
Not quite. It's rule-based keyword matching against the directory data, not a hosted AI model. It handles common phrasing like "I need a plumber" or "what's happening this weekend," but it won't understand everything a true AI assistant would.
What's actually on this site?
A searchable directory of ... local businesses, a community events calendar, transit schedules for all 6 Airdrie routes (no PDF downloads), garbage and recycling pickup days for all 44 neighbourhoods, a list of free and low-cost programs, daily weather with road closures, and a plain-language search box, all in one place.
How is my privacy protected?
You can browse the entire directory, calendar, and resources without creating an account or submitting any personal information. If you use one of our forms, we only use what you submit to respond to your request, and we never sell your information. Full details are in our Privacy Policy.

Parks & outdoor spaces

Chinook Winds Regional Park

55+ acres with 2km of paved pathways. Three playgrounds, a skate park, spray park, beach volleyball, ball diamonds, a winter toboggan hill, and seasonal concessions.

Nose Creek Regional Park

20 acres right in the downtown core. Amphitheatre, concession area, fire pit, picnic tables, a covered shelter, and nature trails.

Fletcher Regional Park

A playground, three shale ball diamonds, and a nine-hole disc golf course.
